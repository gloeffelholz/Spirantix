Emery

The Companion · She remembers.

Memory care, powered by your own life

Meet Emery

For someone living with dementia, Alzheimer's, or short-term memory loss, the real challenge is getting through a normal day. Emery is AI-powered memory support built for exactly that: what's happening today, what needs doing, and who's who. The name is its own mnemonic: one sound away from "memory," easy to call out when you need it.

Emery keeps each day on track. She manages your calendar and task list, builds you a simple daily plan, and gives gentle reminders so nothing falls through the cracks: appointments, medications, birthdays, the kettle you put on.

She also helps you hold on to people and moments. Take or upload a photo and simply ask: "Who is this?" Emery recognizes family and friends from a private knowledge base built from your own photo library, and answers warmly and conversationally.

Family members or caregivers set it up once with the photos, names, and routines that matter; from then on it's as simple as asking. Everything stays on the family's own infrastructure. Nothing is shared, sold, or used for training.

"What's my plan for today?" "What's happening tomorrow?" "Who is this?" "Show me pictures of Sophie" "Remind me to take my pills at 8"

Capsa

The Curator · She keeps the stories.

Your photos, your albums, your stories. In one place you control.

Meet Capsa

Your photos are scattered across Google Photos, iCloud, Dropbox, old laptops, and external drives. Capsa, named for the case Romans used to protect the scrolls they cared enough to keep, gathers all of them into one organized collection on hardware you own, sorted by year, event, and the people in them.

Then comes the part no shoebox of prints could do. Build albums the way you once did by hand, and add the piece no photo can capture: record yourself telling the story. The Slideshow feature plays your pictures with your own voice narrating, in your words, for your grandchildren and theirs.

The AI sees only small thumbnails, just enough to sort intelligently. Your full-resolution photos never leave your machine, with optional cloud backup as a second copy, never the master.

Addie

The Guardian · She knows what's real.

Prove it's real. Prove it's yours.

Meet Addie

In a world filling up with AI-generated images, Addie certifies what's genuine. Hand her a photo or document and she seals it: a visible code, woven into the picture itself, that proves who created it, when it existed, and that it hasn't been altered since.

The seal carries your story with it: who is in the picture, where it was taken, what it meant. Add to that story over years and every version stays linked to the original, so nothing is ever lost or rewritten.

Use Addie on her own to certify a single precious photo, or let her quietly certify everything in your Capsa collection, so the pictures you pass down can be proven real by your great-grandchildren.

Certify a photo or document Survives copies, posts & print Your story travels inside Verifiable for generations

The Tessera Protocol

Every seal Addie creates can be read without us. Tessera is the open, published standard for verifying a sealed file: anyone, with any tool, in any decade, can check that a photo is genuine and unchanged. In ancient Rome, a tessera hospitalis was a token split between two families; decades later, the matching halves could be rejoined to prove the bond. Tessera works the same way for your photos and stories, and it will keep working long after us. That's the point.
